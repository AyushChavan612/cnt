Relevance of Projects in VIT

System Design Project (SDP)

Relevance: SDP is crucial for integrating knowledge from various courses to build a comprehensive system. It simulates real-world software development lifecycles, teaching students about architecture, deployment, and full-stack development.

Engineering Design & Innovation (EDI)

Relevance: EDI focuses on the 'Maker' culture. It encourages students to move beyond theory and build physical or complex software prototypes. It is essential for developing an entrepreneurial mindset and understanding product feasibility.

Design Thinking (DT)

Relevance: Design Thinking is not just about coding; it is about problem-solving. It teaches students to Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. This framework is vital for creating user-friendly products that actually solve human problems.

Course Projects

Relevance: These are subject-specific implementations (e.g., creating a chat app for Computer Networks). They reinforce the specific concepts learned in a single semester and ensure practical competency in that subject.
